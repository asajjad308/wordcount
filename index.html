Frequently Asked Questions

How does the word counter work? ‚Üì
Our tool splits your text by spaces and punctuation to count words accurately. Characters are counted individually, including spaces. Sentences are detected by periods, exclamation marks, and question marks. Everything runs in your browser ‚Äî there's no server involved.
Does it work for SEO content writing? ‚Üì
Absolutely. The keyword density feature shows you which words appear most often, helping you optimize content naturally. The reading time estimate helps you match target page lengths for blog posts, landing pages, and articles.
What is lexical density? ‚Üì
Lexical density is the ratio of unique "content" words to total words. A higher density (above 50%) generally indicates more informative, complex writing. Academic writing typically scores 40‚Äì60%, while conversational text scores lower.
How is reading time calculated? ‚Üì
We use the standard average of 238 words per minute for silent reading and 150 words per minute for speaking aloud ‚Äî both widely cited in reading research. These are approximations; individual reading speeds vary.

The Science of Readability

We implement the Coleman-Liau Index to calculate the grade level of your text. This index relies on character counts and sentence length rather than syllables, making it highly accurate for digital content. A lower score (Grade 7-9) is ideal for general web audiences, while higher scores (Grade 12+) are better suited for academic and technical papers.
